Interesting Insights on the Data

Here we are going to present data analysis we made prior to creating visualizations, to understand what we can do with the data.

Recommendation by Schools Over Years

While looking at the data provided by Bridge To Calculus, we discovered there are multiple schools enrolled in BtC. We thought it would be great if there was a measure that could compare each school. The above graph illustrates the average attendance per year for each school, and it allows users to select specific schools they want to see. There are some schools enrolled for all 5 years, and some are enrolled for just a year. The graph shows that the schools with students enrolled in Bridge to Calculus for all 5 years and with the highest average attendance rate are Boston Community Leadership Academy (orange line) and The English High School (pink line).

Recommendation Distributions Among Schools

Continuing the comparison between schools, we concluded showing the distribution of recommendations at each school can illustrate what type of education each school should focus on. The Y-axis on the graph is the number of recommendations, and the number of recommendations with the length of the full column equal to the number of students enrolled in BtC. This graph can be used to analyze students` general math level. For example, the left-most column, The John D. O`Bryant School of Mathematics, shows that the majority of students were recommended to take AP Calculus AB. The fourth column from the left, Boston Community Leadership Academy, shows there is no student who is recommended Pre-Calculus. By analyzing these statistics, schools and BtC can educate their students more efficiently.

Total Recommendation

This graph shows the distribution of recommendations, with the size of each recommendation being proportional to the number of recommendations. This graph is useful when determining what subjects students mostly need help with. According to the graph, AP Calculus AB carries the biggest portion of recommendations, followed by Pre-Calculus. With this graph in mind, Bridge To Calculus should prepare more teachers for AP Calculus AB and Pre-Calculus than Regular Calculus or AP Calculus BC.

School Distribution

If Bridge To Calculus were to expand locally, figuring out the schools where students actively engage the most would be beneficial. To accomplish that, we made a visualization that shows the top 10 schools with the highest average attendance rate. We added a condition to schools to have at least 5 students because there were some schools with only one or two students. The visualization can be utilized in marketing Bridge To Calculus in the targeted school regions. In addition, it can be easily reversed, show which schools are particularly vulnerable and target the schools with low attendance rate with different approaches.

Students Enrollment Over Years

The best way to prove the effectiveness of Bridge To Calculus is by comparing the grades of students` math scores before and after their enrollment in Bridge To Calculus. However, we do not have that data, so we concluded that the best alternative is to show the number of students enrolled per year. This graph directly shows the size of the group, and a constant upper-right trend will indicate the group`s growth rate. The data shows a decline in the number of students in 2019, followed by slight growth in 2021. (There was no Bridge to Calculus program in 2020 due to the pandemic)
