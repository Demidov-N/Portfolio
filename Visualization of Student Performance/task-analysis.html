As we had a relatively simple dataset, we created a mostly straightforward set of tasks. Our audience is primarily potential donors to Bridge to Calculus so we wanted to present the data in a way that they can see who their donations are benefitting. We were unable to get grades data, so unfortunately we can only show how many people attended and what they were recommended to take, but those are still valuable metrics. We were able to answer Task 1 and 2 with just this data, but were somewhat limited in terms of what we could show for Task 3. The strength of the organization is simply that most of the attendees are recommended for higher-level calculus classes then they would have otherwise taken. We also had two lower-priority "discover" tasks that aren't necessary per se but provide some useful information. Task 4 shows where students are coming from, which can be a cool visual, if nothing else. Task 5 is a bit of an odd one because our small collection of five years doesn't show much in the way of growth, but that's still worth noting.
