Final Visualization

The distance between a point and the point below it on this stacked area chart represents the number of recommendations given in that category in that year. For example, in 2016 there were 20 students recommended to take Pre-Calculus, you get this by subtracting the top point of the yellow shape represeneting Pre-Calculus from the bottom point. On the other hand, the total number of students in the program for each year is reprsented by the topmost point on each graph.

Click and drag to select years with a brush, click outside of the brushed area to reset. This limits the years for the other two charts. When the strip plot is brushed, the stacked area chart shows only that data. You can select increasingly fine areas by brushing them one after the other; to reset to the original area, click outside of the brushed area on both.
This strip plot shows the number of students who had a given level of attendance and received the same recommendation. The position on the y-axis indicates the attendance level of those students, the position on the x-axis indicates the recommendation they received, and the size of each circle represents the number of students with the same attendance and recommendation. The colors encode the same values as the x-axis; they were added to pair with the stacked area chart, which the strip plot is linked to.

Click and drag to select circles with a brush, click outside of the brushed area to reset the data. The data within the area of the brush is the data shown on the other two charts. Selecting years in the stacked area chart will show data in the strip plot for only those years, a change noted by the strip plot's title.
This line chart represents the average attendance rate of schools. The black dotted line represents the overall average of all schools combined. Schools represented with circles are schools that participated in BtC for just a year. Click on the color legend on the right to include/exclude certain schools. Hover over a line or circle to see the name of the school. Brushing on either or both of the stacked area chart and strip plot filters the data shown on this chart, as well.
